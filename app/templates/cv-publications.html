Employment and Education

  • Visiting/Research Fellow (2012 - current)

    Kidney Disease Branch, NIDDK, NIH

    My work involves developing novel diagnostics and therapies for the detection, grading, and treatment of acute kidney injury and chronic kidney disease. I have taken discoveries from in vitro and in vivo models through to pilot studies in humans working with clinical collaborators.

    Recent projects include automated quantification of fibrosis in histology sections and analysis of blood pressure and glomerular filtration rate time series data.

  • Instructor (2015 - 2017)

    Foundation for Advanced Education in the Sciences at NIH

    Co-taught the BIOF309 Introduction to Python course. Developed the syllabus and taught the BIOF509 Machine Learning and Object-Oriented Programming with Python course.

  • PhD Student (2007 - 2012)

    Centre for Cardiovascular Science, University of Edinburgh

    The exosomal proteome as a source of biomarkers for human disease.

    Supervisor: Dr James Dear

    Project Synopsis:

    • Validated and implemented an exosome purification strategy from human urine
    • Systematically investigated factors mediating the release of exosomes from an in vitro murine cortical collecting duct cell model
    • Identified exosomes as being present in adult human cerebrospinal fluid (CSF)
    • Investigated the urinary and CSF exosomal proteomes using tryptic digest and whole protein LC-MS/MS

  • MSc Student (2006 - 2007)

    Cardiovascular Science Program, University of Edinburgh

    Dissertation project: Endothelial cell microparticles as biomarkers of cell activation and dysfunction in sepsis.

    Spring project: The Gap Junction Potentiating Peptide Rotigaptide Does Not Augment the EDHF Response in Rat Mesenteric Arteries.

    Autumn project: Oxysterol exposure does not modulate 11Î²-HSD1 expression in 3T3-L1 adipocytes.

  • BSc Student (2002 - 2006)

    Biochemistry Program, University of York

    Broad based degree in Biochemistry with the third year spent working in industry at GlaxoSmithKline.
